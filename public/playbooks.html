911 Emergency

Possible Stroke

  • Call 911 immediately.
  • Note the exact time symptoms started.
  • Do not give food, drink, or extra medication unless instructed by emergency professionals.
  • Bring medication list, allergy list, insurance card, and ID.
911 Emergency

Chest Pain / Heart Emergency

  • Call 911 right away.
  • Have the patient sit or lie down and stay calm.
  • Unlock the front door if possible.
  • Gather current medications and insurance information.
Urgent

Fall / Possible Fracture

  • Do not force standing if fracture is possible.
  • Call 911 for severe pain, head injury, confusion, or inability to move safely.
  • If stable, contact urgent care or the primary doctor for same-day guidance.
  • Write down what happened, when, and who witnessed it.
Urgent

Medication Reaction

  • Call 911 for trouble breathing, facial swelling, or collapse.
  • Stop the suspected medication unless a doctor tells you otherwise.
  • Take a photo of any visible rash if safe.
  • Keep the medication bottle available for EMS or the doctor.
Admin / Same Day

Medication Running Out

  1. Call the pharmacy and confirm remaining refills.
  2. Call the prescribing office and request an urgent refill.
  3. If prior authorization is needed, ask for the authorization staff member.
  4. Write down the name of each person you speak to and the time of the call.
